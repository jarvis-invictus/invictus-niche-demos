What Is Pharmaceutical Compounding?

Compounding is the science and art of preparing personalised medications for patients whose needs cannot be met by commercially manufactured drugs.

  • Standard medications come in fixed doses — compounding removes that constraint, allowing exact calibration to your prescription.
  • Patients with allergies to dyes, preservatives, or fillers receive formulations stripped of those ingredients.
  • Children who cannot swallow tablets can receive the same medication as a flavoured suspension or gummy.
  • Discontinued or hard-to-source medications can be re-formulated to ensure treatment continuity.
  • Hormone replacement therapy can be bioidentical, matching your body's own hormone profile with precision.
