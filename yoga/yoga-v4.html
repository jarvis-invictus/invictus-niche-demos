Our Story

Born from a mother's
own search for safety

In 2019, Dr. Meera Kulkarni was 26 weeks pregnant and desperately looking for a prenatal yoga class in Pune that wasn't just "regular yoga, but go easy." She wanted instructors who understood diastasis recti, round ligament pain, and the difference between a safe hip opener at 30 weeks versus one that could strain the symphysis pubis.

She couldn't find one. So she built it.

Meera left her physiotherapy practice, earned her Yoga Alliance RPYT (Registered Prenatal Yoga Teacher) certification, trained under Dr. Geeta Iyengar's methodology, and opened MamaMoves in a sunlit 1,200-sqft space in Kharadi. Every sequence we teach is reviewed by an OB-GYN panel. Every prop — from the bolsters to the wall ropes — was chosen for pregnant bodies.

Our Classes

Designed for every stage
of your journey

Your body at 10 weeks is completely different from your body at 34 weeks. Our classes respect that — no one-size-fits-all sequences.

Trimester 1

Foundations & Nausea Relief

The first trimester is about survival — and that's okay. This class focuses on anti-nausea breathing techniques, gentle pelvic floor activation, and restorative poses that help you rest when fatigue hits. We skip inversions and deep twists entirely. If you need to lie on a bolster for the entire hour, that's a perfectly valid class.

🕐 50 min 💆 Very gentle 📅 Weeks 6–13 ₹800/session
Trimester 2

Strength & Stability Flow

The "golden trimester" — you've got your energy back and your belly is growing. This class builds the strength you'll need for labour: deep squats (with wall support), modified warriors, side planks for core stability, and hip openers that prepare the pelvis. We introduce ujjayi breathing as a tool for managing contractions later.

🕐 60 min 💪 Moderate 📅 Weeks 14–27 ₹800/session
Trimester 3

Birth Preparation & Calm

Your body is working hard and needs gentleness now. This class is half movement, half breathwork. We focus on optimal fetal positioning (hands-and-knees sequences), perineal stretching education, birth-ball exercises, and guided visualisation for labour. Partners are welcome to join the last 20 minutes for couples breathing practice.

🕐 60 min 🧘 Gentle + Breath 📅 Weeks 28–40 ₹900/session
Postnatal

Postnatal Recovery & Rebuild

The "fourth trimester" is real, and your body needs rebuilding — not bootcamp. This class starts at 6 weeks postpartum (8 weeks post-C-section, with doctor clearance). We focus on diastasis recti healing, pelvic floor rehabilitation, upper back release for breastfeeding posture, and gentle core reconnection. Babies are welcome — we have a soft play corner.

🕐 50 min 👶 Baby-friendly 📅 6 weeks post-birth ₹800/session
Why Prenatal Yoga

What the research says
(and what our mamas feel)

Prenatal yoga isn't just stretching — it's clinically shown to reduce complications, shorten labour, and improve outcomes for both mother and baby.

🫁

Easier Breathing & Less Anxiety

Pranayama techniques taught in our classes reduce cortisol levels by up to 15%. Mamas report sleeping better within the first two weeks and feeling less overwhelmed by the physical changes.

💪

Shorter, Stronger Labour

A 2020 study in the Journal of Maternal-Fetal Medicine found that women who practised prenatal yoga had 40% shorter first-stage labour. Our mamas consistently report feeling more prepared and in control during delivery.

🦴

Less Back Pain & Swelling

Targeted hip openers and gentle spinal work directly address the postural changes of pregnancy. Our modified cat-cow and side-lying sequences are specifically designed for the shifting centre of gravity in the third trimester.

🧠

Better Mood & Mental Health

Prenatal depression affects 1 in 7 women. Regular yoga practice has been shown to reduce depressive symptoms by up to 30%. Our guided meditation sessions build emotional resilience for the postpartum period.

🤱

Pelvic Floor Readiness

Your pelvic floor is the unsung hero of pregnancy. We teach conscious engagement and release — not just Kegels — so your pelvic floor is strong enough for birth and recovery, without becoming hypertonic.

🤝

A Community That Gets It

Pregnancy can feel isolating. Our small batches (max 8 women) become tight-knit groups. Many of our mamas form lasting friendships — we've had WhatsApp groups running since our first batch in 2019.

Your Safety Comes First

What we do differently
to keep you safe

🛡️

Pregnancy yoga is not the same as regular yoga. Certain poses, breathing patterns, and stretches that are perfectly safe for non-pregnant bodies can be harmful during pregnancy. Here's how we ensure your safety:

  • ✓ Medical intake form required. Before your first class, we collect your OB-GYN details, gestational age, and any complications (gestational diabetes, preeclampsia risk, placenta previa). No form, no class — no exceptions.
  • ✓ No supine poses after 20 weeks. Lying flat on your back can compress the vena cava and reduce blood flow to the baby. After week 20, all our sequences use left-side-lying or inclined positions instead.
  • ✓ No deep twists, no closed-belly poses. We never compress the abdomen. All twists are open twists. All forward folds are wide-legged to accommodate your belly.
  • ✓ Temperature-controlled studio. Our studio is maintained at 23–25°C. We do not offer hot yoga or heated classes for pregnant women. Overheating during pregnancy is a clinical risk.
  • ✓ Emergency protocols in place. All instructors are trained in prenatal first aid. We have a direct line to Dr. Sonal Mehta (OB-GYN, Jehangir Hospital) for any in-class concerns. A first-aid kit with BP monitor is on-site at all times.
  • ✓ Relaxin-aware sequencing. The hormone relaxin loosens your joints during pregnancy, making you more flexible — but also more prone to injury. We intentionally limit range of motion in hip openers and avoid overstretching.
