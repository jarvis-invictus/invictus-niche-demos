Our Story

Born in the Lanes of Sadashiv Peth

We grew up hearing the hum of the shehnai through open windows during wedding season. When founder Smt. Usha Deshpande started Mangalsutra Wedding Co. in 2006, she had one belief — that a Maharashtrian wedding must feel exactly as it has for centuries: fragrant with tulsi and turmeric, alive with the sounds of the taasha-pathak, and anchored in every ritual that connects a family to its ancestors.

Eighteen years later, we've guided over 850 families through their most sacred days — and we still personally oversee every pandit booking, every marigold string, every saat phere.

The Sacred Journey

Your Wedding Ceremony Timeline

A traditional Maharashtrian Hindu wedding is a 3–4 day celebration. Here's how we guide your family through every sacred step.

🍬
Week Before · Day 1

Sakhar Puda — The Engagement

The families formally meet. The bride is presented with sugar (sakhar) wrapped in a silk cloth — a symbol of the sweetness to come. Horoscopes are exchanged and muhurata is confirmed with our resident jyotishi.

Ganesh Puja Sagaai Horoscope Matching
🌿
2 Days Before · Day 2

Haldi & Mehendi Ceremony

Turmeric paste (haldi) is applied to the bride and groom by family members to purify and bless. The evening brings the mehendi — intricate designs drawn while women sing traditional ovi songs.

Haldi Mehendi Ovi Geet
🎶
Day Before · Day 3

Halad & Sangeet Evening

A second turmeric ceremony brings both families together. The night belongs to the Sangeet — live music, the dhol-taasha pathak, and traditional Lavani and Powada performances that bring the community alive.

Halad Sangeet Dhol-Taasha Lavani
🔱
Wedding Day · Morning

Muhurata Pooja & Lagnapatrika

At the auspicious hour determined by our pandit, the wedding puja begins. The Lagnapatrika (wedding invitation) is formally read aloud, and Lord Ganesha blesses the ceremony. The groom arrives in the Baarat procession.

Ganesh Puja Lagnapatrika Baarat Antarpat
🔥
Wedding Day · Main Ceremony

Saptapadi — The Seven Sacred Steps

The heart of the Hindu wedding. Bride and groom take seven steps (pheras) around the sacred fire, each step a vow. The Mangalashtaka is chanted, the mangalsutra is tied, and sindoor is applied — the couple is now united for life.

Saptapadi Mangalashtaka Mangalsutra Kashi Yatra
🏡
Wedding Day · Evening

Reception & Griha Pravesh

The reception follows — a grand feast (pankti bhojan) served on banana leaves. The bride then enters her new home in the Griha Pravesh, welcomed with the Laxmi Pooja and an aarti that officially begins her new life.

Pankti Bhojan Reception Griha Pravesh Laxmi Puja
