FAQ

Questions We Hear Every Day

Straight answers — no marketing fluff.

My dog is 3 years old. Is it too late to train? +

Absolutely not. The phrase "you can't teach an old dog new tricks" is a myth. Adult dogs often train faster than puppies because they have better focus and emotional regulation. We've successfully rehabilitated dogs up to 9 years old at AlphaPaws.

Do you use punishment or shock collars? +

Never. AlphaPaws is 100% force-free and Fear Free certified. We use positive reinforcement, marker training, and relationship-based methods. Punishment-based training creates anxiety and suppresses behaviour without addressing the root cause — we don't do that.

How long before I see results? +

Most owners notice a meaningful shift within the first 2–3 sessions. Consistent, lasting results typically emerge by week 4–5. Every dog is different, which is why we do an assessment first and set realistic, specific milestones for your dog.

Do you offer home visits or only centre-based training? +

Both. Our Behavioral Rehabilitation program includes home visits because real-world environments are essential for dogs with anxiety or territorial issues. Puppy and Obedience programs run at our Baner centre, though we can discuss home sessions for specific situations.

What breeds do you work with? +

We work with all breeds — from Labradors and Golden Retrievers to German Shepherds, Rottweilers, Indie dogs, Beagles, Cocker Spaniels, and more. Our methods are adapted to each breed's instincts, drives, and temperament. No breed is "untrainable."

What if my dog doesn't improve? Do you offer a guarantee? +

Yes. If your dog doesn't meet the agreed training milestones by program end, we offer complimentary additional sessions at no charge. We've only had to invoke this guarantee a handful of times in 9 years — and in every case, the dog succeeded with the extra support.
