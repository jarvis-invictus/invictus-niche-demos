Our Story

A Home Built on
Stories & Stone

Three generations of warmth, one lovingly restored mansion

In 1928, my great-grandfather laid the first stone of this house on what was then a quiet lane off Jangali Maharaj Road. It wasn't meant to be a hotel — it was a family home, built for gatherings, conversations over chai, and monsoon evenings on the verandah.

When I took over the restoration in 2019, my only rule was simple: keep the soul. Every archway, every teakwood railing, every mosaic tile was preserved or faithfully recreated. We added modern comforts — rain showers, Egyptian cotton, air purification — but the bones of the house remain exactly as they were.

Today, The Indigo Residence is what it has always been: a home. Just one that we now share with guests who appreciate the difference between sleeping somewhere and staying somewhere.

Aarav Deshpande

Owner & Third-Generation Custodian

Local Experiences

Pune, Through Our Eyes

Shivajinagar sits at the crossroads of old and new Pune. Here's what's at your doorstep.

3.5 km · 12 min drive

Aga Khan Palace

Mahatma Gandhi's historic prison during the Quit India movement. Italian arches, sprawling lawns, and a profound sense of history.

1.2 km · 5 min walk

FC Road & Deccan

Pune's heartbeat — bookshops, street food, Vaishali's dosa, Goodluck Café's bun maska. Walk there from our front door.

35 km · Day Trip

Sinhagad Fort Trek

A sunrise trek to the Lion's Fort. We'll pack a thermos of chai and fresh pohe for the summit. Ask us to arrange a guide.

Dining

The Verandah Kitchen

Honest food, honestly made

Chef Priya Kulkarni grew up three streets away. She cooks the food she was raised on — Puneri misal with a kick that'll wake you up better than coffee, delicate puran poli that melts on the tongue, and a thali that changes with the seasons because that's how her grandmother did it.

We don't do buffets. Every meal is cooked to order, served on brass thalis, with ingredients sourced from farms in Mulshi and Velhe. Breakfast is included with every stay.
