FAQ

Questions Pune Property
Buyers Ask Us Most

Straight answers to the things every homebuyer in Pune wants to know before hiring a property lawyer.

At minimum: Sale/Title Deed, 7/12 Extract (for non-city areas), Encumbrance Certificate (EC), Commencement Certificate, Occupancy Certificate, RERA registration of the project, building plan approval, NA order, and society share certificate. We can review all of these as part of our title verification service — covering the complete 30-year chain at the IGR Pune office.
Under the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016, homebuyers can file complaints at MahaRERA (Maharashtra's RERA authority) at maharerait.maharashtra.gov.in. Grounds include delayed possession, structural defects within 5 years, false representations, refusal to give the agreement for sale, etc. We handle the entire process — drafting the complaint, presenting evidence, and representing you at hearings. Most complaints are resolved within 60–180 days.
Absolutely yes — in fact it's even more critical. Even for RERA-registered projects, the underlying land might have litigation, agricultural land restrictions, multiple ownership claims, or undisclosed mortgages taken by the builder. Your bank will also typically require a clean title opinion letter before disbursing a home loan. We've seen cases in Baner, Wakad, and Sus Road where new projects had serious title issues. Don't skip this step.
For Pune Municipal Corporation area: Stamp duty is 6% for men and 5% for women (plus 1% LBT Metro Cess), and registration fee is 1% of the market value subject to a maximum of ₹30,000. Rates vary slightly for PCMC jurisdiction. These are calculated on the Ready Reckoner (circle rate) or actual sale consideration, whichever is higher. We help calculate your exact liability and ensure correct stamp duty is paid to avoid penalties.
Yes, NRIs holding Indian passport can purchase residential and commercial property in India under FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act). They don't need prior approval from RBI. However, specific FEMA reporting, power-of-attorney formalities, TDS compliance (1% from the sale consideration for NRI sellers), and repatriation rules apply. We regularly assist NRI clients buy and register property remotely in Pune without requiring a visit.
Deemed conveyance is a Maharashtra-specific legal remedy that transfers the land title from the builder to the housing society when the builder refuses or delays executing the conveyance deed. Societies can apply to the Competent Authority under MCS Act. We have successfully obtained deemed conveyance for several Pune societies. The process typically takes 4–8 months. We handle the entire application, hearing, and registration process on your society's behalf.
