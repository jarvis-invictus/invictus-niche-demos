Ritucharya — Seasonal Living

Wellness Through the Seasons

Live in rhythm with nature — Dr. Priya's seasonal tips for Pune's climate.

☀️

Grishma Ritu

Summer • March – May

Pune's dry heat aggravates Pitta. Focus on cooling foods, hydration, and gentle routines to stay balanced.

  • Drink kokum sherbet, aam panna, and tender coconut water daily
  • Apply Chandan (sandalwood) paste on pulse points before sleep
  • Avoid heavy exercise between 11 AM – 4 PM; prefer early morning walks
  • Include gulkand (rose petal jam) in your diet to cool Pitta
🌧️

Varsha Ritu

Monsoon • June – September

Pune's heavy monsoons weaken Agni (digestive fire) and aggravate Vata. Warm, light food is your best friend.

  • Sip warm water with ginger and tulsi throughout the day
  • Avoid raw salads, curd, and cold foods — prefer warm, cooked meals
  • Apply warm sesame oil to joints on cold rainy mornings
  • This is the ideal season for a Panchkarma detox program
❄️

Hemant Ritu

Winter • November – February

Pune's pleasant winters are when Agni is strongest. Nature wants you to nourish deeply during this season.

  • Enjoy warm milk with turmeric, saffron, and a pinch of nutmeg at night
  • This is the best season for Abhyanga — regular oil massage strengthens immunity
  • Include ghee, dates, almonds, and seasonal root vegetables in meals
  • Wake early and practice Surya Namaskar to harness winter sun energy
🌸

Vasant Ritu

Spring • February – March

The transition season in Pune. Kapha accumulated in winter begins to melt — allergies and sluggishness peak.

  • Start your morning with warm honey-lemon water to clear Kapha
  • Favour light, dry, and warm foods; reduce dairy and sweets
  • Incorporate Triphala powder at bedtime for gentle cleansing
  • Nasyam therapy is especially powerful during this season for allergies
